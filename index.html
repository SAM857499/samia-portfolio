Case Study 01 · IoT Platform
ALTECH: Privacy-First Smart Home Platform
MVP development for an offline-capable IoT automation platform — Saudi residential market
🔒 app.altech.sa/dashboard
Product Dashboard · Arabic-First Interface
Status
Pre-Launch · Q2 2026 Target
Team
4 Engineers · 2 Designers · 1 QA
Market
Saudi Arabia Residential
The Problem
Saudi homeowners faced privacy concerns around cloud-dependent IoT, connectivity gaps, and unacceptable latency in real-time automation. No solution combined offline-first architecture with local data sovereignty and an Arabic-first UX.
Key Decisions
  • 15+ user interviews revealed privacy as the #1 adoption barrier — repositioned "offline-first" as a privacy feature, not a technical limitation
  • Worked with engineering to architect local processing with cloud sync only when available — achieving sub-100ms latency vs. 500ms+ in cloud-dependent systems; metrics currently being validated pre-launch
  • Scoped MVP to 4 device types and 8 core automations using RICE prioritization to meet Q2 2026 deadline
  • Ensured compliance with Saudi data residency laws, electrical standards, and building codes
Key Learning
Privacy is a product feature, not a compliance checkbox. Repositioning offline-first as a user benefit shifted interview sentiment dramatically — homeowners expressed willingness to pay premium for data sovereignty.

Case Study 02 · Enterprise SaaS
FAAINEX: Enterprise HR Automation
Cloud-based HR platform with OCR automation and full Saudi labor law compliance — 5,000+ active users
🔒 app.faainex.com/compliance
Compliance Dashboard · Qiwa & Mudad Integration
Duration
9 Months (Part-Time)
Active Users
5,000+
Key Integrations
Qiwa · Mudad · WhatsApp
The Problem
Saudi enterprise HR teams manually tracked Qiwa and Mudad compliance, processed paper documents, and operated across disconnected tools — causing compliance errors, penalties, and slow employee onboarding.
Key Decisions
  • 50+ stakeholder interviews surfaced Qiwa/Mudad compliance as the highest-pain, highest-value feature — prioritized in Q1 roadmap using RICE
  • Designed WhatsApp OCR flow: employees submit documents via WhatsApp → AI extracts data → auto-populates HR records, reducing manual data entry significantly
  • Defined data contracts for Qiwa and Mudad API integrations — became the platform's strongest competitive moat
  • Streamlined sprint planning to achieve 3 consecutive on-time releases
Key Learning
Compliance, treated as a product feature rather than overhead, becomes a premium differentiator. Enterprise clients chose FAAINEX specifically because it automated their biggest operational burden.

Case Study 03 · B2B SaaS
Metas Research Studio: Scaling B2B SaaS
Growing a B2B SaaS platform to 3,000+ users across 10+ international markets with a lean team of 7
🔒 app.metas.studio/growth
Growth Dashboard · Analytics & A/B Testing
Duration
Oct 2023 – Dec 2025
Users Achieved
3,000+
Markets
10+ International
The Challenge
Scaling a B2B SaaS platform from early adoption to international growth while maintaining product quality, team cohesion, and product-market fit — all with a lean 7-person team.
Key Decisions
  • Built Mixpanel analytics stack to track activation, retention, and feature adoption KPIs — gave the team a shared language for decisions
  • Behavioral analytics identified a key funnel drop-off in the trial experience; redesigned it and improved activation-to-paid conversion
  • Ran systematic A/B tests on onboarding flows, achieving 20% uplift in engagement and 10% reduction in churn
  • Made a strategic ICP pivot — narrowed from broad market to SME/mid-market, eliminating feature sprawl and doubling sprint velocity
Key Learning
The ICP pivot was the most important decision. Short-term discomfort of de-prioritizing some users paid off in sharper positioning, faster iteration cycles, and stronger PMF with our core segment.
