The Situation
ALTECH was developing a privacy-focused IoT smart home automation platform for the Saudi residential market. The sector demanded real-time performance yet operated under strict data sovereignty expectations and intermittent connectivity. High cloud costs and latency risks threatened both unit economics and user trust from day one.
My Role & Mandate
As Senior Product Owner, I owned the full MVP vision, scope, and go-to-market strategy across four device types, eight core automations, and an Arabic-first interface — with a hard Q2 2026 launch target and a lean team of six.
Discovery & Insight

Through 15+ in-depth interviews with Saudi homeowners, I found that privacy was the primary buying signal — far stronger than convenience features. What the team saw as a technical limitation was actually a cultural and regulatory differentiator in the Kingdom's evolving data-protection climate.

The Decision & Tradeoff

I repositioned "offline-first" as the flagship privacy feature rather than a cost-saving compromise. We deprioritized cloud-only advanced automations in MVP to protect delivery velocity, betting that premium privacy positioning would accelerate adoption and willingness-to-pay.

Advanced automations delayed → Privacy narrative accelerated PMF
Execution Highlights
  • Architected local-processing infrastructure delivering <100ms latency in pilots — no cloud round-trips required.
  • Translated research insights into prioritized backlog and Arabic-first UX patterns from day one.
  • Aligned engineering, design, and leadership around the privacy-first narrative — turning constraint into core value proposition.
Retrospective
What I'd do differently
Bring legal and regulatory stakeholders into discovery even earlier to stress-test the privacy narrative against evolving Saudi data-protection laws — further de-risking GTM before MVP scope commitment.

The Situation
FAAINEX needed to differentiate in a crowded enterprise HR SaaS market by automating Saudi labor-law compliance while reducing manual workload. Legacy processes were error-prone, slow, and increasingly non-compliant with Qiwa and Mudad mandates — creating real legal exposure for customers.
Discovery & Insight

50+ interviews with HR leaders revealed compliance headaches and fragmented data entry were the biggest time sinks. The real gap wasn't AI — it was the lack of seamless, trustworthy integration with government platforms like Qiwa and Mudad.

The Decision & Tradeoff

I prioritized native integrations (Qiwa, Mudad, WhatsApp OCR) over broader feature expansion. Analytics and reporting features were explicitly delayed until compliance workflows were rock-solid — because trust and accuracy were the real moat in the regulated Saudi enterprise segment.

Analytics delayed → Compliance trust secured first
Execution Highlights
  • Defined clean data contracts and compliance workflows across engineering, legal, and integration partners.
  • Streamlined backlog to protect 3 consecutive on-time releases despite part-time remote constraints.
  • Translated 50+ interviews into automation KPIs the entire team could rally around.
What I'd do differently
Invest earlier in a lightweight customer advisory board to validate edge-case compliance scenarios before finalizing integration scope.

The Situation
Metas was serving a broad, fragmented customer base spanning micro-businesses to mid-market enterprises. Feature requests were overwhelming the team, delivery velocity was declining quarter-on-quarter, and we were spreading resources too thin across mismatched audience segments — building for everyone, resonating with no one.
Discovery & Insight

Mixpanel behavioral analytics revealed engagement and conversion diverted sharply between micro-business and mid-market users. Drop-off patterns confirmed micro-businesses churned at activation while mid-market users retained and expanded. We were building for everyone and resonating with no one.

The Decision & Tradeoff

I drove a deliberate ICP narrowing to SME and mid-market only. Walking away from some existing micro-segment revenue was uncomfortable — but it allowed us to eliminate feature sprawl, deepen value for the core audience, and regain the velocity needed to compete internationally.

Short-term micro-segment revenue → Mid-market depth and velocity restored
Execution Highlights
  • Redesigned onboarding and trial experience based on Mixpanel drop-off points → directly lifted activation-to-paid conversion.
  • Implemented OKR + RICE frameworks to align 7-person team around strategic focus.
  • Led iterative UX improvements that drove measurable behavioral changes across 10+ international markets.
What I'd do differently
Run a 6-week time-boxed pilot of the narrowed ICP with a small cohort before full commitment — to quantify revenue impact earlier and reduce internal pushback.
